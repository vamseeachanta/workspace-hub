# /competitive-brief - Create Competitive Analysis Brief

Research competitors and generate a structured competitive analysis.

## Usage

```
/competitive-brief <competitor name(s)>
```

## Inputs

1. **Competitor name(s)** (required)
2. **Your company/product context** (optional): what you sell, positioning, differentiators
3. **Focus areas** (optional): messaging, product/features, content, news, pricing, market presence

## Workflow

### 1. Research

For each competitor, research:
- Company website: homepage messaging, product pages, pricing
- Recent news: press releases, funding, launches, partnerships (last 6 months)
- Content strategy: blog topics, resource types, social presence
- Review sites: third-party comparisons, customer review themes
- Job postings: hiring signals for strategic direction (optional)

### 2. Generate Brief

**Competitor Profiles**: Overview, messaging analysis, product positioning, content strategy, strengths, weaknesses

**Messaging Comparison Matrix**: Primary tagline, target buyer, key differentiator, tone/voice, core value prop

**Content Gap Analysis**: Topics and formats covered across competitors

**Opportunities**: Positioning gaps to exploit, underserved segments

**Threats**: Areas of competitive vulnerability

**Recommended Actions**: 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations with quick wins and strategic moves

### 3. Follow-Up

Offer to:
- Create sales battlecards
- Draft messaging exploiting positioning gaps
- Dive deeper into specific competitors
- Set up competitive monitoring

## Key Principles

- The most valuable part is the "so what" -- strategic implications
- Be honest about competitor strengths
- Customer reviews are gold for insight
- Date-stamp everything -- competitive intelligence has a short shelf life
