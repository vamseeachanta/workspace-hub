# /competitive-brief - Marketing Competitive Analysis

Research competitors and generate a structured competitive analysis comparing positioning, messaging, content strategy, and market presence.

## Usage

```
/competitive-brief <competitor name(s)>
```

## Inputs

1. **Competitor name(s)** (required)
2. **Your company/product context** (optional): what you sell, positioning, key differentiators
3. **Focus areas** (optional): messaging, product/features, content, news, pricing, market presence

## Research Process

For each competitor, research using web search:
1. Company website -- homepage messaging, product pages, pricing page
2. Recent news -- press releases, funding, product launches, partnerships (last 6 months)
3. Content strategy -- blog topics, resource types, social presence, webinars, podcasts
4. Review sites -- third-party comparisons, analyst mentions, customer review themes
5. Job postings -- hiring signals indicating strategic direction (optional)

## Output Structure

1. **Executive Summary**: 2-3 sentence overview, biggest opportunity and threat
2. **Competitor Profiles**: Overview, messaging analysis, product positioning, content strategy, strengths, weaknesses
3. **Messaging Comparison Matrix**: Tagline, target buyer, differentiator, tone, value prop
4. **Content Gap Analysis**: Topics and formats across competitors
5. **Opportunities**: Positioning gaps, messaging angles, underserved segments
6. **Threats**: Competitive strengths, favorable trends, recent moves
7. **Recommended Actions**: 3-5 specific recommendations with quick wins and strategic moves

## Follow-Up Options

- Create sales battlecards
- Draft messaging exploiting positioning gaps
- Dive deeper into specific competitors
- Set up competitive monitoring plan
