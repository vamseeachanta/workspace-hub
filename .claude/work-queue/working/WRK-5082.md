## Mission

Calculate the force in the parachute frame from the launch/deployment of a parachute on a drag car.

## What

## Known Gaps in Existing 2D Model

**INCOMPLETE:** The existing `frame_model.py` / `frame_solver.py` only models the **rear trunk frame** (Page 1 geometry — horizontal bar + V-struts, 6 nodes, 6 members). The **front horizontal bar under the hood** (Page 2 geometry — curved member, B1 bolted connection with 6 bolts, P1 pinned BC) is NOT modelled.

The under-hood frame is part of the load path — it transfers load from the trunk frame through the vehicle chassis to the front subframe. Without it, the current 2D model treats the trunk frame endpoints (C3) as fully fixed, which overestimates the fixity and underestimates deflections/stresses at those connections.

**Action:** Child 1 (3D CAD) must capture the full frame geometry (trunk + under-hood). Child 4 (2D analysis) must extend `build_gt1r_frame()` to include the under-hood members before the analysis is considered complete.

## Objective 1 — Load Distribution Through Strut Frame

Assess how the parachute drag load transfers from the chute attachment point through the strut frame members to the vehicle mounting points. This must be understood before stress checks.

Key questions to resolve:
- Load path from chute line → bracket → tubular arm → triangulated frame → body mount points
- Which members are in tension, compression, or bending at each load case
- Reaction forces at each body mount bolt location
- Whether the frame behaves as a truss (axial only) or frame (axial + bending)
