## Mission

Extend the propeller-rudder literature survey (WRK-1148) with 4-quadrant propeller performance data, enabling crash-stop, crash-astern, and full maneuvering envelope modelling.

## What / Why

The current literature (WRK-1148) covers only 1st-quadrant operation (forward motion, forward thrust: J > 0). Real maneuvering scenarios include:

- **Crash-stop**: engine reversed while ship moves forward (2nd quadrant)
- **Crash-astern**: engine driving astern, ship moving astern (3rd quadrant)
- **Transient backing**: ship moving forward, propeller braking (4th quadrant)

Without 4-quadrant K_T(β) and K_Q(β) data (where β = atan(V_A / (0.7π·n·D))), the WRK-1150 implementation cannot model emergency maneuvers — a critical gap for safety analysis.

### Key sources to gather:
- van Lammeren, van Manen & Oosterveld (1969): Wageningen B-series 4-quadrant data
- Oosterveld & van Oossanen (1975): systematic B-series polynomials
- ITTC benchmark propellers with 4-quadrant curves
- Carlton (2007) Chapter 4: 4-quadrant representation
