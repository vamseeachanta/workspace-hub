## Mission

Research, assess, and implement calculation frameworks for drilling riser analysis — covering stackup/tension, operability envelopes, VIV fatigue, and structural response — suitable for fast-running engineering assessment.

## What / Why

Drilling risers are critical path equipment for offshore drilling operations. The riser connects the subsea BOP stack to the drilling vessel and must withstand combined environmental, operational, and vessel motion loads. Key analysis areas include:

- **Riser stackup and tension** — joint inventory, buoyancy sizing, top tension requirements across water depth range
- **Operability envelopes** — vessel offset limits, current/wave heading combinations, disconnect criteria
- **VIV and fatigue** — vortex-induced vibration response, fatigue damage accumulation at critical joints
- **Structural damping** — parametric influence on dynamic response
- **Tool passage / spacing** — clearance verification for BHA and completion tools

Existing `digitalmodel/src/digitalmodel/drilling_riser/` has modules for damping, operability, stackup, and tool passage — this study will ground those implementations in literature, validate against published methods, and fill gaps.
