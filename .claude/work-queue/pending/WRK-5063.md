## Mission

Research, assess, and implement calculation frameworks for vortex-induced vibration (VIV) analysis — covering screening, response prediction, fatigue damage, and suppression — applicable to risers, pipelines, and tubular members.

## What / Why

VIV is a critical design driver for slender marine structures. The existing `digitalmodel/src/digitalmodel/subsea/viv_analysis/` module has 11 files covering fatigue, screening, vortex shedding, and frequency calculation. This study will:

- **Vortex shedding fundamentals** — Strouhal number, lock-in, reduced velocity, Reynolds number regimes
- **Response models** — empirical (DNV-RP-F105), semi-empirical (SHEAR7, VIVANA), wake oscillator
- **Fatigue from VIV** — S-N curves, Miner's sum, stress range estimation, modal superposition
- **Screening criteria** — onset velocity, in-line vs cross-flow, multi-mode response
- **Suppression** — helical strakes, fairings, effectiveness factors
- **Current profiles** — uniform, sheared, directional; power ratio method (DNV-RP-F105)

Ground existing implementations in published literature and validate against benchmark cases.
