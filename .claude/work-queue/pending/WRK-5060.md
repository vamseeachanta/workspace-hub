## Mission

Research, assess, and implement calculation frameworks for subsea/offshore pipeline engineering — covering on-bottom stability, free span analysis, installation mechanics, lateral buckling, and wall thickness design — suitable for fast-running engineering assessment.

## What / Why

Pipeline engineering requires validated calculation methods across multiple disciplines from design through installation and operation. Key areas include:

- **Wall thickness design** — pressure containment (API 1111, DNV-ST-F101), combined loading, corrosion allowance
- **On-bottom stability** — hydrodynamic loading (DNV-RP-F109), absolute/generalised lateral stability, soil resistance
- **Free span analysis** — static and dynamic response, VIV screening (DNV-RP-F105), fatigue from free span VIV
- **Lateral buckling** — Hobbs' analytical solutions, controlled buckling (snake-lay, sleepers), post-buckling strain
- **Installation mechanics** — S-lay and J-lay catenary, stinger tip departure angle, residual curvature, abandonment/recovery
- **Expansion and walking** — pipeline axial expansion, walking mechanisms, end expansion calculation

Existing `digitalmodel/docs/domains/pipelines/` has reference materials covering buckling, free span, and installation topics. This study will establish the formal literature base, select validated methods, and implement them.
