## Mission

Research and prepare a calculation methodology to study the hydrodynamic interaction between a ship's propeller and rudder across the full engine operating envelope: engine-off, engine startup, and drive-off from slow to full RPM.

## What

Develop a calculation framework that models propeller-rudder interaction across three operating regimes, all parameterised by propeller RPM:

1. **Engine-off condition** — n = 0; propeller free-wheeling or locked; rudder operates in wake deficit
2. **Engine startup / low RPM** — n low; propeller begins generating thrust; slipstream forms and grows; advance ratio J transitions from high to low
3. **Drive-off: slow to fast** — n ramps from idle to full rated RPM; propeller thrust and torque increase; slipstream velocity (axial + tangential) at rudder plane evolves as a function of n and ship speed V

Key relationship: advance ratio **J = V / (n · D)** links ship speed, RPM, and propeller diameter — governing the transition from lightly-loaded (high J) to heavily-loaded (low J) propeller as RPM rises during drive-off.

## Why

Understanding propeller-rudder interaction is critical for:
- Accurate maneuvering performance across the full RPM range (departure, slow-speed manoeuvring, transit)
- Rudder force and torque estimation as propeller loading changes during drive-off
- Ship design studies: stopping, crash-stop, berthing, and normal transit scenarios

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Literature review of propeller-rudder interaction models (momentum theory, vortex methods, CFD-based correlations)
- [ ] Mathematical formulation for engine-off case: free-wheeling or locked propeller wake deficit affecting rudder lift/drag
- [ ] Mathematical formulation for engine-on case: propeller slipstream velocity (axial + tangential) at rudder plane as function of shaft RPM and delivered power
- [ ] RPM-sweep capability: calculation can be evaluated at discrete RPM steps from 0 to rated RPM to trace drive-off transient
- [ ] Advance ratio J computed and used throughout; KT/KQ from open-water diagram or regression
- [ ] Calculation implemented as a Python module or script (in `digitalmodel` or standalone)
- [ ] Worked numerical example with representative ship parameters (propeller diameter, rated RPM, rudder area, ship speed)
- [ ] Results documented as tables/plots: rudder forces vs RPM, slipstream velocity vs RPM, J vs RPM at representative ship speeds
