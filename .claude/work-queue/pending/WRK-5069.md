## Mission

Research, assess, and implement calculation frameworks for subsea production risers and flowlines — covering steel catenary risers (SCR), flexible risers, hybrid risers, and subsea flowline design — complementing the pipeline engineering study (WRK-5060) with production-system-specific methods.

## What / Why

Subsea risers and flowlines differ from export pipelines in their dynamic loading, thermal-pressure cycling, and connection to floating production systems. Existing code spans `subsea/catenary_riser/`, `subsea/vertical_riser/`, `subsea/catenary/`, and `assetutilities/calculations/` (SCR fatigue, riser dynamics, riser VIV):

- **SCR design** — catenary shape, TDP fatigue, touchdown zone soil interaction, slugging effects
- **Flexible risers** — bend stiffener design, minimum bend radius, annulus flooding, fatigue endurance
- **Hybrid/free-standing risers** — buoyancy can design, tether tension, dynamic response
- **Flowline design** — thermal insulation, cool-down time, hydrate management, slug catcher sizing
- **Riser-vessel interface** — hang-off loads, flex joint angle, stroke capacity
- **Fatigue at TDP** — soil-riser interaction, trench formation, S-N fatigue accumulation
