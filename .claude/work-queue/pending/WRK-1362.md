## Mission

Calculate parachute drag force for all applicable load cases including single and dual chute configurations per Stroud sizing charts.

## What

Existing `parachute_drag.py` calculates drag for a single chute. Per the Stroud sizing data in WRK-5082:
- **Single chute**: speeds up to ~200 MPH (model 430 Std. 32 for 3200-4000 lbs)
- **Dual chutes**: speeds over 200 MPH (models 430-28/430-30/450/470 for 2800-4000 lbs)

Need to assess both configurations and determine which applies at each speed.

## Why

GT1R R35 at 3,600 lbs:
- At 200 MPH: single chute may be adequate (Stroud 430 Std. 32)
- At 250 MPH: dual chutes required per Stroud chart (430-28 or 430-30 Pro-Mod range)
- Frame must be designed for the governing load case

## Scope

1. Review Stroud sizing charts — confirm single vs dual threshold for 3,600 lb vehicle
2. Calculate drag for single chute at 200 and 250 MPH (existing capability)
3. Calculate drag for dual chute at 250 MPH — two chutes, each with own Cd and area
4. Determine governing load case for frame design
5. Document chute parameters (diameter, Cd, Cx) with source references

## Sensitivity Analysis

Assess aerodynamic lift and tire traction at deployment speeds:
- Calculate aero lift on R35 at 200 and 250 MPH (F_lift = 0.5*rho*V^2*Cl*A_frontal)
- Compute effective tire normal force = W - F_lift + F_downforce
- Compute available friction = μ × N_effective (μ ~0.8 dry, ~0.5 wet)
- Compare chute drag vs available friction — assess tire lift-off risk
- If F_drag > friction capacity → frame mounts see full chute load as pure tension/shear

Need: Cl for R35 body (CFD, wind tunnel, or published estimates). GT1R package may include splitter/wing.
