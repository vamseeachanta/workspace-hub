## Mission

Create a skill that combines visionary product thinking with disciplined project management to identify the highest-value next task — balancing internal harness/tooling work against actual engineering deliverables — with a bias toward market readiness for the oil and gas industry. The skill should think like the best product visionaries (Jobs, Musk, Ive) — ruthlessly cutting what doesn't matter, obsessing over what the end user actually experiences, and saying no to 100 things so we can say yes to the one that changes the game.

## What

A strategic prioritization skill that replaces ad-hoc "what's next?" decisions with a structured framework. It should:

1. **Classify work into tracks**:
   - **Harness** — work-queue, skills, CI/CD, agent tooling, ecosystem plumbing
   - **Engineering** — digitalmodel, assetutilities, worldenergydata, assethold, OGManufacturing calculations/features
   - **Market readiness** — demos, documentation, aceengineer-website, presentation artifacts, industry-facing outputs

2. **Score and rank** pending WRK items against:
   - Market impact (does this move us closer to presenting to O&G industry?)
   - Engineering value (does this build reusable calculation capability?)
   - Harness necessity (is this blocking engineering or market work?)
   - Dependency chain (what does this unblock?)
   - Effort vs payoff ratio

3. **Enforce a healthy balance** — harness work is necessary but should not dominate; the skill should flag when harness WRKs exceed a configurable threshold (e.g. >40% of active work) and redirect toward engineering/market items

4. **Race-to-market lens** — every recommendation should answer: "How does this help us present our engineering tools to the oil and gas industry sooner?"

5. **Output a prioritized shortlist** — top 3-5 items across all tracks with rationale, not just a sorted list

6. **Visionary filter** — apply product visionary principles to every recommendation:
   - **Jobs: "Focus is about saying no"** — actively recommend WRK items to defer, descope, or kill. A 450-item queue is a symptom of saying yes to everything. The skill should trim ruthlessly.
   - **Jobs: "Start with the customer experience and work backwards"** — an O&G engineer using our tools should feel delight, not see our internal plumbing. Prioritize what the user touches.
   - **Musk: "First principles thinking"** — when scoring, ask "does this need to exist at all?" before asking "how important is it?" Eliminate before prioritizing.
   - **Ive: "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication"** — prefer one polished, demo-ready module over five half-finished ones. Depth over breadth.
   - **Grove: "Only the paranoid survive"** — identify the strategic inflection point: what is the one capability that, if we ship it now, changes how the industry sees us?
   - **Bezos: "Work backwards from the press release"** — for market-track items, frame the recommendation as "what would the announcement say?" If there's no compelling announcement, it's not market-ready work.

7. **The "demo in 30 seconds" test** — every top recommendation must pass: "Can an O&G engineer see the value of this in a 30-second demo?" If not, it's infrastructure, not product.

## Why

- The workspace has ~450 WRK items; without strategic filtering, harness/tooling work absorbs all capacity
- The actual value is in engineering modules (CP, fatigue, hydrodynamics, energy data, asset analysis) — these are what the O&G industry cares about
- A marketing milestone (conference, demo, website launch) creates a forcing function that aligns all work streams
- The skill encodes product management discipline so every session starts with "what moves the needle most?" rather than "what's next in the queue?"
- Great products are built by people who say no more than they say yes — this skill is the mechanism for saying no to harness work that doesn't serve the end user
- The O&G industry judges tools by what they do, not how they're built — our internal sophistication means nothing if the demo isn't compelling

## Integration Points — Where This Skill Gets Invoked

### 1. Session Start (`session-start/SKILL.md`)
- **When:** Every session, after repo-sync and before any work begins
- **How:** Automatically called as the final step of session-start; replaces or augments the current "top unblocked item per category" logic
- **Output:** The session opens with a strategic brief — not just "here's what's pending" but "here's what matters today and why"

### 2. `/whats-next` skill (`workspace-hub:whats-next`)
- **When:** User explicitly asks "what should I work on?"
- **How:** Strategic overlay on top of the existing blocker-resolution and priority sorting; adds the visionary filter and track balance report
- **Output:** Enriched `/whats-next` output with track ratios, "say no" list, and 30-second demo test results

### 3. `/work run` — before WRK pickup (`work-queue/SKILL.md` Stage 1)
- **When:** User triggers `/work run` and the system selects the next item from the queue
- **How:** Instead of pure FIFO or priority-only ordering, the strategic prioritization score determines which pending WRK gets picked up next
- **Output:** Selected WRK includes a one-line strategic rationale ("picked because: unblocks CP demo for OTC conference")

### 4. `/work add` — at capture time (`work-queue/SKILL.md` capture phase)
- **When:** A new WRK is being captured
- **How:** Quick classification into harness/engineering/market track; lightweight first-principles check ("does this need to exist?"); auto-tag with track
- **Output:** New WRK gets `track:` field in frontmatter; warning if harness ratio is already over threshold

### 5. WRK Stage 3 — Triage (`work-queue-workflow` triage stage)
- **When:** A WRK item enters triage
- **How:** Full scoring against all criteria (market impact, engineering value, dependency chain, effort/payoff); visionary filter applied
- **Output:** Triage decision enriched with strategic score and track classification

### 6. Weekly/periodic review (`/today`, `/reflect`)
- **When:** During daily or weekly reviews
- **How:** Track balance report showing harness vs engineering vs market ratios over time; trend analysis — are we drifting toward tooling?
- **Output:** Dashboard-style summary: "This week: 60% harness, 30% engineering, 10% market — REBALANCE NEEDED"

### 7. WRK Stage 15 — Future Work Synthesis
- **When:** A WRK is closing and generating follow-up items
- **How:** Future work items are pre-scored and pre-classified before they enter the queue; visionary filter kills low-value follow-ups at birth
- **Output:** Only high-value follow-ups enter `pending/`; the rest go to a `deferred/` or `maybe-never/` bucket

### 8. Feature WRK decomposition (Stage 7 exit)
- **When:** A complex feature is being broken into child WRKs
- **How:** Each child WRK is scored for market impact; decomposition is rebalanced to front-load user-visible children over internal plumbing children
- **Output:** Child WRK ordering biased toward "demo-able first, infrastructure last"
