## Mission

Research, assess, and implement calculation frameworks for core drilling engineering — covering torque and drag, hydraulics, well control, casing design, and drill string mechanics — suitable for fast-running engineering assessment.

## What / Why

Drilling engineering calculations underpin well planning, real-time operations, and post-well analysis. Key areas requiring a literature-grounded calculation framework include:

- **Torque and drag** — soft-string and stiff-string models, friction factor estimation, buckling onset (Dawson, He-Kyllingstad)
- **Drilling hydraulics** — equivalent circulating density (ECD), pressure loss models (Bingham, Power Law, Herschel-Bulkley), bit hydraulics optimisation
- **Well control** — kick tolerance, shut-in pressures, kill sheet calculations, volumetric methods
- **Casing design** — burst/collapse/tension (API 5C3, ISO 10400), biaxial/triaxial design factors, connection ratings
- **Drill string mechanics** — critical rotary speed, lateral vibrations, weight-on-bit transfer

Existing `digitalmodel/docs/domains/drilling/` has reference materials but no structured calculation modules. This study will establish the literature base, select validated methods, and implement them.

## Acceptance Criteria
