## Mission

Plan and scope CFD analysis for the drag car parachute deployment scenario — vehicle aerodynamics, parachute flow interaction, time-marching transient at 250 MPH deployment, and impact load extraction.

## What

Develop a CFD analysis plan addressing four questions:

### Q1: Car Drag, Lift, and Tire Traction Forces

**Existing capability:** `chute_assessment.py` already calculates:
- Aerodynamic lift: `L = 0.5 * rho * V^2 * Cl * A_frontal` (R35 defaults: Cl=0.35, A=25 ft²)
- Tire traction: `N_eff = W - F_lift + F_downforce`, `F_friction = mu * N_eff` (mu=0.8)
- Load cases at 200 and 250 MPH with liftoff risk detection

**Gap:** These use simplified coefficients. CFD would provide spatially resolved pressure distribution, more accurate Cd/Cl at high speed, and ground effect modeling. Plan should define whether simplified coefficients are sufficient or CFD-derived values are needed.

### Q2: Parachute Aerodynamics (Existing Assets)

**Existing capability:** `parachute_drag.py` implements:
- `F = 0.5 * rho * V^2 * Cd * A * Cx` (22 test cases, opening shock Cx=1.2-1.8)
- Single and dual chute configurations
- Stroud sizing chart lookups

**Gap:** Current model is quasi-static (steady drag at a given speed). Does not model:
- Canopy inflation dynamics (squidding → partial → full inflation)
- Wake interaction between dual chutes
- Vehicle wake effect on chute Cd
- Porosity-dependent Cd variation during opening

### Q3: Time-Marching Transient Analysis at 250 MPH Deployment

**Existing capability:**
- OrcaFlex dynamic simulation framework (`execute_dynamic_analysis()` with configurable timestep/duration)
- OpenFOAM infrastructure (domain builder, mesh, post-processing)

**Plan scope:**
- Transient simulation from deployment (t=0, V=250 MPH) through full inflation
- Vehicle deceleration profile: `m * dV/dt = -F_drag_car - F_drag_chute(t)`
- Chute inflation curve: empirical Cx(t) or CFD-derived inflation profile
- Time history of all forces (drag, lift, traction, chute pull)
- Key output: peak force, time-to-peak, deceleration g-load profile

### Q4: Impact Load and Associated Forces

**Existing capability:**
- Opening shock factor Cx in parachute_drag.py (static multiplier)
- Frame solver handles static equivalent loads

**Plan scope:**
- Dynamic amplification factor (DAF) for frame response
- Impulse load calculation: `J = integral(F(t) dt)` over snatch interval
- Peak vs quasi-static force ratio
- Whether frame analysis needs dynamic FEM (transient CalculiX) or static equivalent with DAF suffices

## Approach Options (to evaluate in planning)

| Option | Fidelity | Effort | Tools |
|---|---|---|---|
| A: Enhanced analytical | Medium | Low | Extend parachute_drag.py with inflation curve Cx(t), coupled 1-DOF vehicle decel ODE |
| B: Coupled analytical + OrcaFlex | Medium-High | Medium | Analytical chute force → OrcaFlex time-domain frame response |
| C: Full external CFD | High | High | OpenFOAM external aero (car body) + porous canopy model |
| D: Hybrid (A for forces + C for validation) | High | Medium-High | Analytical for design, CFD for spot-check validation |
