## Mission

Research, assess, and implement calculation frameworks for coiled tubing (CT) engineering — covering hydraulics, fatigue life, buckling, weight-on-bit transfer, and operational limits — suitable for fast-running engineering assessment.

## What / Why

Coiled tubing is widely used for well intervention, drilling, and cleanout operations. Existing `digitalmodel/src/digitalmodel/marine_ops/ct_hydraulics/` has a basic hydraulics module. This study will establish a comprehensive literature base:

- **CT hydraulics** — friction pressure loss in coiled pipe (Dean number correction), annular pressure loss, circulating density
- **CT fatigue** — low-cycle plastic fatigue, reel diameter effects, pressure cycling, fatigue life tracking
- **Buckling** — helical and sinusoidal buckling in CT, Dawson-Paslay criterion, lockup depth
- **Weight transfer** — drag in deviated wells, CT vs jointed pipe friction, WOB at BHA
- **Operational limits** — collapse pressure (API 5ST), tensile capacity, depth limits, speed limits
