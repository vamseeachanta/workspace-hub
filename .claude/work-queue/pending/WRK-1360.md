## Mission

Model the full parachute mounting frame from hand sketches and client photos as a 3D CAD assembly in FreeCAD — tube members, connections, and vehicle mount points.

## What

Create a 3D stick/wireframe model of the GT1R R35 parachute frame covering both structural assemblies:

1. **Rear trunk frame (Page 1):** Main horizontal bar (36" total: 6"+12"+12"+6" segments), 7.25" vertical offset, V-shaped triangulated struts converging at coupler pin. Connections C0 (weld), C1 (bolt+pin, shear), C2 (weld), C3 (weld, fixed BC at frame rails).
2. **Under-hood frame (Page 2):** Horizontal bar (1/2" thick, 5" span), curved member at 150° included angle. Connections B1 (6x bolted to chassis/subframe), P1 (pinned BC).

Superimpose tube cross-sections (4130 chromoly, OD/wall TBD — pending client measurements) onto the stick model for section properties.

## Why

The 3D CAD model is the geometry source for all downstream analysis children (2D drawings, 2D frame analysis, 3D FEM, OrcaFlex). Without it, 5 of 9 children are blocked.
