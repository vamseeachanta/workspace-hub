Prompt Start Context

Review The Well — a 15 TB open physics simulation dataset collection (16+ datasets, pip-installable Python API, PyTorch DataLoader, Hugging Face streaming) — and produce a structured evaluation identifying which datasets, benchmark models, and tooling are worth integrating into the workspace-hub ecosystem. Output: an entry in specs/online-resources/catalog.yaml (WRK-391) plus a standalone evaluation note at specs/online-resources/the-well-evaluation.md.

Why

The Well is a curated, high-quality corpus of physics simulation data covering fluid dynamics, magnetohydrodynamics, acoustic scattering, turbulence, and reaction-diffusion systems. Several of these domains directly overlap with current and planned modules:

  • Fluid dynamics (Rayleigh-Bénard, Euler equations, shear flow, turbulence) — training data and benchmarks for CFD-adjacent ML modules on ace-linux-2
  • Rayleigh-Taylor instability, viscoelastic instability — relevant to structural and materials integrity modelling
  • MHD (64 and 256 resolution) — relevant to electromagnetic and corrosion physics
  • Acoustic scattering — relevant to subsea inspection and NDE (non-destructive evaluation)
  • Planetary SWE (shallow water equations) — metocean / oceanographic wave modelling crossover; potential supplement to worldenergydata/metocean/
  • Benchmark suite (FNO and other neural operator models) — ready-made ML baselines that could seed ML capability in digitalmodel or a future ml-engineering module

The pip package (pip install the_well) and Hugging Face streaming make integration low-friction — no bulk download required to prototype.
