## Key Strategic Decision
Position A&CE as **"AI-native engineering insights & analytics for offshore energy"** (a FIRM), not "Vamsee the AI-native engineer" (a PERSON).

### Why Firm > Person
- Clients buy from firms — easier to justify $120K retainers to procurement
- Eliminates key-man risk perception
- Acquirers pay more for "company with IP" than "talented individual to hire"
- Comparable companies (Cognite, Akselos, Palantir) all positioned as platforms/firms

### The Sequence
- Phase 1 (now): Firm positioning + win 3-5 paying clients. Revenue is oxygen.
- Phase 2 (parallel): Build Vamsee's personal brand as the expert BEHIND A&CE.
- **Sell the firm. Promote the founder.**

## Comparable Companies
- **Cognite** — Industrial data platform, $225M raised, acquired by Aker. Started as domain expertise + software, positioned as platform from day one.
- **Akselos** — Structural performance management, $16.5M raised. "1,000x faster, 10x more accurate." Platform positioning.
- **Palantir** — Framed as analytics platform (tech multiples) even though heavily services-dependent.

## The Steinberger Parallel (Context, Not Blueprint)
Peter Steinberger (OpenClaw creator) got acquired by OpenAI. But his path — 13 years bootstrapping PSPDFKit → exit → 43 side projects → OpenClaw goes viral — required a previous exit for financial security and a horizontal product for viral reach. Vamsee's domain is vertical and specialized. The playbook is Cognite, not Steinberger: **build the firm, sell the firm.**
