Blog Post 6 (candidate): "Slim Hole vs. Standard Hole: Engineering Trade-Offs That Define Well Economics"

  • Source: Radmir Ganiev, PhD — "Drilling Advisor's Logbook" newsletter (Feb 19, 2026)
  • URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/radmirganiev_bighole-slimhole-welldesign-ugcPost-7430142115973779456-4_w-/
  • Key insight: Slim-hole drilling is a complete system redesign, not a diameter reduction — it cascades through casing programs, BHA design, hydraulics, and logistics. ECD becomes hypersensitive to flow rate and rotation; hole cleaning margins shrink.
  • SEO keywords: slim hole drilling, well bore design, ECD management, HYDPRO WELLPLAN, offshore well economics, deepwater ERD drilling
  • Data points to use:
  • ECD sensitivity comparison: slim vs. standard at same depth/mud weight (WRK-218 module)
  • Application matrix: when slim beats standard (narrow pressure window, ERD, capital efficiency)
  • BSEE well data: bore diameter distribution across GoM deepwater wells
  • Software in use: HYDPRO (Pegasus Vertex), WELLPLAN (Halliburton) — position A&CE analysis as validation layer
  • Content angle: Practitioner framing — "Why your hydraulics model becomes non-optional in slim-hole design, and what the trade-off really costs operationally." Decision framework with engineering depth, not vendor marketing.
  • Internal links: engineering.html (drilling analysis services), contact.html
  • Related WRK: WRK-218 (well bore design analysis module)
