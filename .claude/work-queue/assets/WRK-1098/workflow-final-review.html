A workspace-hub:ecosystem-terminology skill (and matching AGENTS.md/CODEX.md/GEMINI.md snippet) that defines:

  • Canonical repo names and their short aliases (e.g. assetutilities not asset_utilities, asset-utilities, or AssetUtilities)
  • Module/package naming within each repo
  • Relationship vocabulary — what "tier-1 repo", "submodule", "hub", "harness" mean in this ecosystem
  • File/directory names that are easily confused (e.g. work-queue vs work_queue, SKILL.md vs skill.md)
  • Acronyms and abbreviations in consistent expanded form (e.g. WRK, GTM, CP, VIV)
  • People/machine names — ace-linux-1, ace-linux-2, acma-ansys05; never ace1, linux-1, etc.
  • Do-not-use list — deprecated names, client codenames, incorrect pluralisations
Why — - Coding assistants regularly hallucinate repo/module names or use inconsistent capitalisation, leading to broken imports and confusing comm...
  • Coding assistants regularly hallucinate repo/module names or use inconsistent capitalisation, leading to broken imports and confusing commits.
  • Humans onboarding to the ecosystem have no single reference — terminology is scattered across CLAUDE.md, MEMORY.md, and various docs.
  • A skill that is always loaded (or easily invokable) eliminates both problems at source.
