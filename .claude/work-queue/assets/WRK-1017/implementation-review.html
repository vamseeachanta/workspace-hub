Mission

Identify why agents skip the Stage 5 user-agent interactive planning session and enforce it as a hard gate.

Scope note: this WRK is intentionally a multi-deliverable workflow-governance refactor, not a narrow one-file bug fix. Closure requires both the Stage 5 hard-gate rollout and the Stage 7 review-packaging obligations already accepted into scope.

What

Stage 5 of the 20-stage work-queue lifecycle is defined as an interactive agent-user plan session (not a one-way artifact drop). Agents routinely skip the interactive walk-through and jump straight from plan creation to Stage 6 cross-review or Stage 7, bypassing the collaborative review and decision-capture that Stage 5 requires.

Why

Stage 5 is where scope in/out, acceptance criteria adjustments, risks, and constraints are collaboratively resolved. Skipping it means plans proceed with unvalidated assumptions, leading to implementation drift, rework, and scope creep — exactly the problems the lifecycle was designed to prevent.

Status

  • Stage 5 user approval is complete for the current published baseline.
  • Stage 6 cross-review is still in progress.
  • In this WRK, plan_approved: true with plan_reviewed: false means the Stage 5 baseline is approved and published, but the plan is not yet cleared by Stage 6.
  • If Phase 1B hard-gate rollout clears before Phase 2 packaging is finished, the WRK status remains pending with Stage 6/7 open; it does not move to implementation or close until Stage 7 packaging is complete.

  • Preserve the full Stage 5 target flow in this WRK:

  • Preserve model-specific effort wording and mandatory per-pass session-log/evidence updates.

  • Preserve degraded-mode fallback policy as part of making the 3-provider flow operational.

  • These decisions must not be narrowed or removed without explicit user approval.
