## Mission
Prevent mid-WRK context exhaustion by monitoring the budget and auto-checkpointing
before the session dies, so work is always resumable without lost progress.

## What
- Hook or script: `scripts/hooks/context-monitor.sh` — reads Claude's context usage
  signal (available via session log or hook event) and emits a structured warning
  log entry when usage crosses 70% and 80% thresholds
- At 80%: auto-call `scripts/work-queue/checkpoint.sh` for the active WRK item
  (reads `.claude/state/active-wrk`) and write a `context-warning.yaml` into assets/
- Session chunking guide: `.claude/docs/session-chunking.md` — practical rules for
  splitting large WRKs into resumable sub-sessions (max artifact per session,
  natural break points, what to write to checkpoint before stopping)
- Add context-budget awareness to the WRK lifecycle: Stage 10 execution notes
  recommended chunk size by complexity (simple <40%, medium <70%, complex = multi-session)

## Why
Context exhaustion mid-Stage 10 is the most common WRK interruption. Currently
agents continue past safe limits, produce truncated output, and require manual
`/wrk-resume` reconstruction. Auto-checkpoint preserves state and makes resumption
deterministic.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] `context-monitor.sh` emits structured log at 70% and 80% thresholds
- [ ] Auto-checkpoint fires at 80% for active WRK (reads active-wrk state)

- [ ] `.claude/docs/session-chunking.md` covers: when to chunk, break-point patterns,
