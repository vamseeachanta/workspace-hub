# Marine Incident Taxonomy & Classification System

## Overview

This document defines the comprehensive taxonomy for categorizing and analyzing marine incidents across all data sources.

---

## 1. Incident Categories (Primary Classification)

### 1.1 Collision & Contact
- **Collision with Vessel** - Contact with another ship
- **Collision with Fixed Object** - Contact with structures (bridges, platforms, piers)
- **Striking/Allision** - Vessel strikes object (includes berthed vessels)
- **Struck Submerged Object** - Underwater obstacles
- **Struck Floating Object** - Debris, logs, containers

### 1.2 Grounding & Stranding
- **Grounding (Power)** - Under power/propulsion
- **Grounding (Drift)** - While drifting
- **Stranding** - Vessel stuck ashore/shoal

### 1.3 Flooding & Foundering
- **Flooding (Progressive)** - Gradual water ingress
- **Flooding (Sudden)** - Rapid water ingress
- **Foundering** - Vessel sinking
- **Capsizing** - Vessel overturning
- **Listing** - Severe vessel tilt

### 1.4 Fire & Explosion
- **Fire (Engine Room)** - Machinery space fires
- **Fire (Accommodation)** - Living quarters
- **Fire (Cargo Hold)** - Cargo area fires
- **Fire (Other)** - Other locations
- **Explosion (Fuel)** - Fuel-related explosions
- **Explosion (Cargo)** - Cargo explosions
- **Explosion (Other)** - Other explosion types

### 1.5 Machinery & Equipment Failure
- **Total Failure (Propulsion)** - Main engine failure
- **Total Failure (Steering)** - Steering system failure
- **Partial Failure (Machinery)** - Degraded machinery
- **Equipment Failure** - Non-propulsion equipment
- **Electrical System Failure** - Power/electrical issues
- **Hull/Structural Failure** - Structural integrity issues

### 1.6 Loss of Control
- **Loss of Directional Control** - Steering issues
- **Loss of Propulsion Power** - Engine power loss
- **Loss of Navigation** - Navigation system failure
- **Drift** - Uncontrolled movement

### 1.7 Human Casualties
- **Falls Overboard** - Person overboard
- **Struck by Motor/Propeller** - Propeller injuries
- **Struck by Boat** - Hit by vessel
- **Falls in Boat** - Falls within vessel
- **Occupational Accident** - Work-related injuries
- **Serious Injury/Death** - Fatalities or severe injuries

### 1.8 Cargo & Stability
- **Cargo Shift** - Cargo movement
- **Overloading** - Excessive cargo/weight
- **Stability Issues** - Balance problems
- **Cargo Damage** - Damaged cargo

### 1.9 Environmental & Weather
- **Heavy Weather Damage** - Storm damage
- **Ice Damage** - Ice-related incidents
- **Flooding/Swamping (Weather)** - Weather-induced flooding

### 1.10 Hazardous Materials
- **Pollution** - Environmental contamination
- **Hazmat Incident** - Hazardous material release
- **Chemical Spill** - Chemical releases

## 2. Root Cause Categories

### 2.1 Human Factors

#### Operational Errors
- **Operator Inattention** - Distraction, lack of focus
- **Operator Inexperience** - Insufficient training/experience
- **Excessive Speed** - Speed too high for conditions
- **Careless/Reckless Operation** - Negligent behavior
- **Sharp Turn** - Sudden course changes
- **No Proper Lookout** - Inadequate watch
- **Rules of the Road Infraction** - Navigation rule violations
- **Restricted Vision** - Obstructed view

#### Judgment & Decision Making
- **Poor Judgment** - Bad decision making
- **Failure to Follow Procedures** - Procedural violations
- **Improper Planning** - Inadequate preparation
- **Navigation Error** - Chart/route errors

#### Fatigue & Impairment
- **Operator Fatigue** - Exhaustion
- **Alcohol Involved** - Alcohol impairment
- **Drug Impairment** - Drug influence
- **Medical Emergency** - Health issues

### 2.2 Equipment & Machinery Failure

#### Propulsion Systems
- **Engine Failure** - Main engine issues
- **Transmission Failure** - Gearbox problems
- **Propeller Failure** - Propeller damage/loss
- **Fuel System Failure** - Fuel delivery issues

#### Control Systems
- **Steering Failure** - Steering mechanism failure
- **Rudder Failure** - Rudder damage/loss
- **Throttle Failure** - Throttle control issues

#### Electrical & Electronics
- **Electrical System Failure** - Power systems
- **Battery Failure** - Battery issues
- **Navigation Equipment Failure** - GPS, radar, etc.
- **Communication Failure** - Radio/communication systems

#### Hull & Structural
- **Hull Failure** - Hull integrity issues
- **Hatch Cover Failure** - Hatch problems
- **Door Failure** - Watertight door issues
- **Seal Failure** - Gasket/seal problems
- **Structural Damage** - Frame/structure issues

#### Safety Equipment
- **Lifesaving Equipment Failure** - Life raft, jackets
- **Fire Suppression Failure** - Fire fighting equipment
- **Bilge Pump Failure** - Pump malfunction

### 2.3 Environmental Factors

#### Weather Conditions
- **Storm/Heavy Weather** - Severe weather
- **High Winds** - Strong wind conditions
- **Heavy Rain** - Reduced visibility
- **Fog** - Visibility < 0.5 nm
- **Snow/Ice** - Winter conditions

#### Sea State
- **Rough Seas** - High waves (>2m)
- **Strong Current** - Current issues
- **Tidal Conditions** - Tidal effects

#### Visibility
- **Poor Visibility** - Visual range limited
- **Darkness** - Night operations
- **Glare** - Sun glare issues

### 2.4 Hazardous Waters

#### Navigation Hazards
- **Submerged Objects** - Underwater hazards
- **Shallow Water** - Depth restrictions
- **Narrow Channel** - Confined waters
- **Congested Waters** - Heavy traffic
- **Uncharted Hazards** - Unknown obstacles

#### Infrastructure
- **Poor Channel Marking** - Inadequate markers
- **Inadequate Lighting** - Poor navigation lights
- **Unmarked Hazards** - No warnings

### 2.5 Maintenance & Condition

#### Vessel Condition
- **Poor Maintenance** - Deferred maintenance
- **Corrosion** - Material degradation
- **Age-Related Deterioration** - Old vessel issues
- **Inadequate Inspection** - Inspection failures

#### Design Issues
- **Design Defect** - Inherent design flaws
- **Inadequate Equipment** - Insufficient equipment
- **Improper Modification** - Unauthorized changes

### 2.6 Operational Factors

#### Loading & Cargo
- **Overloading** - Excessive weight
- **Improper Loading** - Poor weight distribution
- **Cargo Not Secured** - Unsecured cargo
- **Hazardous Cargo** - Dangerous goods issues

#### Operational Procedures
- **Inadequate Procedures** - Poor SOP
- **No Safety Management** - Lack of SMS
- **Inadequate Training** - Insufficient crew training
- **Communication Breakdown** - Poor communication

## 3. Contributing Factors

Multiple factors often contribute to incidents:

### 3.1 Compound Causes
- Primary cause + contributing factor(s)
- Chain of events leading to incident
- Multiple system failures

### 3.2 Severity Modifiers
- **Aggravating Factors**: Weather, congested waters, night operations
- **Mitigating Factors**: Quick response, effective damage control, nearby assistance

---

## 4. Severity Classification

### 4.1 IMO Classification
- **Very Serious Marine Casualty**: Total loss, death(s), or severe pollution
- **Marine Casualty**: Significant damage or injury requiring external assistance
- **Marine Incident**: Minor damage or injury, vessel proceeds

### 4.2 Consequence Types
- **Lives Lost**: Fatalities
- **Serious Injuries**: Hospitalization required
- **Minor Injuries**: First aid only
- **Vessel Loss**: Total constructive loss
- **Major Damage**: Significant repair required
- **Minor Damage**: Cosmetic/minor repairs
- **Environmental**: Pollution/contamination
- **Economic**: Financial loss value

## 5. Analysis Dimensions

### 5.1 Temporal
- Year, Quarter, Month, Season
- Day of week, Time of day
- Watch period (0-4, 4-8, 8-12, etc.)

### 5.2 Geographic
- Region, Country, Port
- Open sea vs. coastal vs. inland
- Traffic density areas
- Specific waterways

### 5.3 Vessel Characteristics
- Vessel type (cargo, tanker, passenger, fishing, etc.)
- Size (gross tonnage, length)
- Age (years in service)
- Flag state
- Classification society

### 5.4 Operational Context
- Phase of voyage (underway, maneuvering, berthing, anchored)
- Cargo status (loaded, ballast, empty)
- Weather conditions at time
- Visibility conditions
- Water depth
