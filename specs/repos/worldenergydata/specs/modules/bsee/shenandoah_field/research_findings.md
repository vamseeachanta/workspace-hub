## Executive Summary

The Shenandoah field represents one of the most financially complex deepwater developments in Gulf of Mexico history, with over **$2.3 billion** in cumulative write-offs and impairments by original partners, followed by a **$1.8 million** bankruptcy sale, and ultimately a **$1.8 billion** redevelopment by new partners. **Production actually began in July 2025**, achieving 100,000 BOPD by October 2025.

### Key Financial Metrics
- **Total impairments/write-offs (2017)**: ~$1.4 billion (Anadarko $902M, Cobalt $232.8M)
- **Bankruptcy sale price (2018)**: $1.8 million (99.9% value loss)
- **Blackstone/Beacon valuation (2020)**: ~$250 million implied
- **FID development cost (2021)**: $1.8 billion
- **Production status**: First oil July 25, 2025; ramped to 100k BOPD by Oct 2025

## 1. DISCOVERY & EARLY APPRAISAL (2009-2014)

### Initial Discovery
- **2009**: Anadarko discovers Shenandoah in Walker Ridge Blocks 51, 52, 53
- **2013**: Shenandoah-2 appraisal well encounters 1,000 feet of high-quality oil pay
- **Initial estimates**: 300-600 million barrels (analysts suggested up to 900 MMbbl)

### Whistleblower Warning (2014)
- **Lea Frye** (Senior Reservoir Engineer at Anadarko) warns executives in 2014 that field is "likely much smaller than claimed"
- Frye provides forthright technical analysis contradicting company's optimistic projections
- Following her warnings, Frye faces workplace harassment and is forced out
- **2016**: Frye files SEC whistleblower complaint alleging fraud

## 2. IMPAIRMENTS & WRITE-OFFS (2017)

### Anadarko Impairment (May 2, 2017)
- **$467 million impairment charge** on Shenandoah assets
- **$435 million expensed** in suspended exploratory well costs
- **Total Anadarko write-off: $902 million**
- Reason: "Disappointing appraisal results and unfavorable commodity prices"
- Suspension of further appraisal activities announced
- Stock price drop followed disclosure

## 3. COBALT BANKRUPTCY & ASSET SALE (2017-2018)

### Bankruptcy Filing (December 14, 2017)
- Cobalt files Chapter 11 in U.S. Bankruptcy Court, Southern District of Texas
- Case No. 17-36709
- $2.8 billion total funded debt
- 100-200 creditors
- Failed sale of Angola assets precipitated filing

- **Total auction proceeds**: $577.9 million for all Cobalt Gulf of Mexico assets
- **Shenandoah sale**: **$1.8 million** to Navitas Petroleum US LLC

- **Other major sales**:

  - 13 exploration blocks: $25M (Total)

## 5. FINAL INVESTMENT DECISION (2021)

### FID Announcement (August 25, 2021)
- **Total development cost**: **$1.8 billion**
- **Phase 1 financing secured**: $900 million
- **Expected start-up**: Q4 2024 (later revised to Q2 2025)
- **Operator**: Beacon Offshore Energy

### Current Ownership Structure (2021+):
- **ShenHai** (Navitas subsidiary): 49.0%
- **Beacon Offshore Energy**: 20.05%
- **HEQ Deepwater**: 20.0% (joined 2021 to help fund development)
- **BOE II Exploration**: 10.95%

### Development Details
- **Location**: Walker Ridge Block 52, 150 miles off Louisiana
- **Water depth**: 1,700-1,900 meters (5,577-6,234 feet)
- **Reservoir depth**: ~30,000 feet
- **Floating Production System (FPS)**: 120,000 BOPD capacity
- **Initial wells**: 4 production wells
- **Technology**: 20,000 psi high-pressure development

### Key Contracts
- **Drilling**: Transocean Deepwater Atlas drillship - $252M (255-day initial program + 275-day completion)
- **Subsea**: Subsea 7 - EPCI contract (tieback of 4 wells via manifold, flowlines, risers)
- **Pipeline**: Williams - 5-mile offshore pipeline to Williams infrastructure
- **Stress Joints**: Hunting PLC - titanium stress joints

## 7. INVESTOR LAWSUITS & LEGAL DISPUTES

### Securities Class Actions Against Anadarko
- **Lead plaintiffs**: Norfolk County Council, Georgia Firefighters' Pension Fund
- **Period covered**: February 20, 2015 - May 2, 2017
- **Allegations**: 
  - Misrepresented Shenandoah's potential following Shen-2 success
  - Failed to disclose Shen-3 was a dry hole
  - Inflated reserve estimates despite internal warnings
  - Executives profited from misleading disclosures
- **Key evidence**: Lea Frye's 2014 warnings and 2016 SEC complaint
- **Status**: Class certification granted, then vacated and remanded by 5th Circuit (2024)

### Executive Compensation Controversy
- **CEO Al Walker**: Received $100 million golden parachute when Occidental acquired Anadarko (2019)
- Investor lawsuit argues executives misled investors to maximize personal exit packages
- Anadarko sold to Occidental for $38 billion despite Shenandoah collapse

### Lea Frye Whistleblower Case
- **Frye v. Anadarko** (5th Circuit, No. 18-20543)
- Claims: Dodd-Frank whistleblower retaliation
- Outcome: Retaliation claim dismissed on procedural grounds, but declaratory judgment on NDA allowed to proceed

## 8. ECONOMIC RECONCILIATION ANALYSIS

### Total Capital Deployed (Cumulative)

2. **Write-offs/impairments (2017)**: $1.4 billion (Anadarko $902M + Cobalt $233M)
3. **Bankruptcy sale (2018)**: $1.8 million recovery (99.9% value loss)
4. **Blackstone acquisition (2020)**: $250 million
5. **FID development (2021-2025)**: $1.8 billion
6. **Phase 2 expansion (2025-2026)**: ~$300-400 million (estimated)

**Total invested across all parties**: >$4 billion

### Value Progression
- **2013-2014 peak valuation**: $4 billion (investor lawsuit reference)
- **2017 post-impairment**: Near zero (complete write-off by Anadarko/Cobalt)
- **2018 bankruptcy sale**: $1.8 million (for 70% WI in Shenandoah prospect)
- **2020 implied valuation**: ~$800 million (based on $250M for 31%)
- **2021 FID valuation**: $1.8 billion development commitment
- **2025 producing asset**: Market value TBD (100k BOPD production)

### Economic Returns Analysis (Projected)
- **Rate of return**: 37% (analyst estimate at FID)
- **Carbon intensity**: Low vs. other regions (cited at FID)
- **Production profile**: 100k-140k BOPD plateau through 2026+
- **Reserve life**: 211 MMBOE / 120k BOPD = ~4.8 years (Phase 1+2 only)

### Winner/Loser Summary
**Losers:**
- Anadarko investors: $902M write-off + stock losses
- Cobalt investors: $233M write-off + bankruptcy
- ConocoPhillips: Unknown losses (30% WI exited)
- Cobalt creditors: Major haircut in bankruptcy

**Winners:**
- Navitas: $1.8M entry for 49% of producing asset
- Blackstone/Beacon: Operatorship + equity in high-return project
- HEQ Deepwater: 20% of producing asset (entry timing/price unknown)
