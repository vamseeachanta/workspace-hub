**Footnote - FPS and Subsea Infrastructure Cost Allocation:**

The FPS and Subsea Infrastructure line items above reflect the following actual contract structure:

**FPS ($700M total):**
- Fabrication contract (Hyundai Heavy Industries, Aug 2021): $576M - Design, procurement, construction, and delivery of semi-submersible FPS to United States
- Owner's costs and margins: $124M - Engineering (FEED), project management, transport from Korea to Ingleside TX (COSCO), commissioning equipment, and contingency

**Subsea Infrastructure ($250M total):**
- Subsea equipment: ~$140M - Subsea trees (TechnipFMC), manifolds, flowlines, risers, umbilicals
- Installation services (portion): ~$110M

**Installation Services (Subsea 7, Jan 2022): $150-300M total contract scope includes:**
- Subsea infrastructure installation (EPIC): Structures, flowlines, risers, umbilicals (~$90-120M estimated)
- FPS wet tow: Towing from Ingleside TX to field location ~150 miles offshore (~$15-25M estimated)
- Mooring system installation: 5,800 ft water depth spread mooring system (~$60-95M estimated)
- FPS hook-up and commissioning: Riser/umbilical connections, system integration (~$30-45M estimated)

*Note: Subsea 7's contract bundles all installation work together. Component cost estimates are based on Gulf of Mexico deepwater project industry benchmarks. Actual cost allocation between subsea infrastructure installation and FPS installation (towing, mooring, hook-up) is not publicly disclosed.*

**Budget Reconciliation:**
- Confirmed contracts: FPS fabrication $576M + Installation $150-300M = $726-876M
- Budget allocation: FPS $700M + Subsea Infrastructure $250M = $950M
- Margin/contingency: $74-224M (8-24% buffer, reasonable for deepwater HPHT project)

For detailed FPS component cost analysis, see: `FPS_COST_BREAKDOWN_ANALYSIS.md`

## PART 7: IRR CALCULATIONS

### Formula

IRR is the discount rate where NPV = 0:

$$NPV = \sum_{t=0}^{n} \frac{CF_t}{(1 + IRR)^t} = 0$$

### Navitas IRR Calculation (Detailed)

**Cashflows:**
- Year 0 (2018): -$1.8M
- Years 1-5 (2019-2023): -$220M, -$220M, -$220M, -$220M, -$2M
- Year 6 (2024): $0
- Years 7-15 (2025-2033): +$213M, +$546M, +$740M, +$696M, +$786M, +$709M, +$608M, +$113M, +$95M

**Using Excel/Python IRR function:**
```python
cashflows = [-1.8, -220, -220, -220, -220, -2, 0, 213, 546, 740, 696, 786, 709, 608, 113, 95]
irr = np.irr(cashflows)
# Result: ~29.2%
```

**Interpretation:**
- Navitas achieves **~29% IRR** on $1.056B invested
- Lower than analyst estimate of 37% but still excellent
- Difference likely due to conservative assumptions (oil price, production profile)

### Beacon IRR Calculation

**Cashflows:**
- Year 0 (2020): -$250M
- Years 1-4 (2021-2024): -$90M, -$90M, -$90M, -$91M
- Years 5-13 (2025-2033): +$86M, +$223M, +$302M, +$286M, +$320M, +$289M, +$247M, +$45M, +$38M

**Using IRR function:**

cashflows_beacon = [-250, -90, -90, -90, -91, 86, 223, 302, 286, 320, 289, 247, 45, 38]

**Interpretation:**

- Solid return but lower than Navitas (entered later, paid more)

### Why Analyst Estimate of 37% IRR May Be Higher

**Possible explanations:**
1. **Higher oil price assumption** ($85-90/bbl vs. our $74/bbl base)
2. **Lower OPEX assumption** ($12-14/BOE vs. our $17/BOE)
3. **Higher production** (optimistic decline curves)
4. **Gas revenue included** (we modeled oil only, gas adds ~10-15%)
5. **Tax benefits** (NOLs, accelerated depreciation not modeled)
6. **Terminal value** (asset sale after 2033 not included)
